Stipend Indexing & Extended Season Pay

A proposal for fair, automatic, and fiscally responsible coach & sponsor compensation

Two Inequities. One Solution.

Problem 1 — Stipends Don't Grow

Stipends are fixed dollar amounts. When the district raises base salaries, coaches & sponsors get nothing. Every contract cycle restarts from zero.

Problem 2 — Playoff Work is Unpaid

Coaches who lead teams deep into the postseason work weeks of extra hours for $0 in additional pay — while ticket takers and chain workers earn $25/hr at the same games.

"We should not financially penalize our most successful educators by asking them to work for a lower effective hourly rate the further they advance."

The Stipend Index

Securing Automatic, Guaranteed Raises

How the Index Works

Current Model

  • Stipends are flat dollar amounts
  • Base salary rises ~1.8%/yr — stipends stay frozen
  • Coaches must fight for increases every contract cycle
  • Pits coaches against classroom teachers for the same pool

Proposed Index Model

  • Each stipend becomes a % of the base salary
  • When base salary goes up, stipends go up automatically
  • No renegotiation needed — it's built into the contract
  • A win for base salary = a win for every coach & sponsor
"A win for base salaries is automatically a win for our high-level program leaders."

Our Proposal

Index from the $50,000 Base (2023-24)
The "Natural Raise" Model

Set index percentages using the original 2023-24 base salary of $50,000.

Effect: Applies an immediate ~5.34% increase for 2026-27 — capturing four years of missed growth while the base salary increased and stipends did not.

Going forward: Every time the district raises the base salary, every stipend rises automatically by the same percentage. No renegotiation needed.

This is not a raise — it is a correction. We are simply asking that four years of professional work be valued the same way as four years of classroom work.

Anticipating Board Objections

Objection 1 — "Budget Creep": Automatic escalators prevent accurate long-term budget forecasting.
Rebuttal: Dollar increases are modest — a head football coach gains ~$200/year. A locked-in percentage improves forecasting; the Board always knows exactly what stipends will cost.
Objection 2 — "Retroactive Jump": Paying a ~5.34% immediate increase is an unbudgeted cash grab.
Rebuttal: This is not a retroactive raise — it is correcting four years of flat pay while the base salary grew. The dollar amounts are modest (a Category D sponsor gains ~$149 in Year 1), and the index creates predictable, forecastable costs going forward.
Objection 3 — "Loss of Flexibility": Automatic raises drain money from classroom resources.
Rebuttal: Historically base salaries win because they affect more people, leaving coaches behind. Linking both eliminates internal infighting — a win for base salary is a win for everyone.

Extended Season Pay

Non-Guaranteed Time. Professional Rate.

The Ticket Taker Inequity

What Happens Today

Ticket sellers, announcers, timers, and chain workers earn $25/hr for game-day event support.

Coaches who lead those same teams into the postseason earn $0 in additional pay — their stipend covers only the regular season.

The Logical Problem

The contract defines the $25/hr rate for duties "which do not require the professional judgment of a licensed educator."

Playoff coaching — advanced strategy, direct student mentorship, high-stakes decision-making — should never be paid at the same rate as selling tickets.

"We are classifying the most professional work in the building at the same rate as the most operational."

The Extended Season Pay Framework

The Rate

$35/hr

The contract's "professional rate" — already used for summer school, driver's ed, and curriculum work. Coaching qualifies.

Overnight / State Trips

$250/day

Per-diem replaces hourly tracking for state trips — covers 24/7 supervision without time-sheet complexity.

Accountability

All hours pre-certified via a Playoff Calendar (e.g., "Standard Playoff Week = 15 hours"). Triggered by a bracket win. Approved by Head Coach + AD.

"Non-Guaranteed Time" — not "Playoff Pay" — because every sport's postseason structure is unique.

Billable vs. Non-Billable Time

Billable Time

  • Practices beyond the regular season (~2 hrs/day)
  • Game day: setup + event + travel block (~6 hrs)
  • Supervising/coaching at state meets or playoff games
  • Travel time to away/overnight state competitions

Non-Billable Time

  • Film review, game planning, preparation work
  • Voluntary Sunday night or off-day team events
  • Unapproved extra practice time (coach's own initiative)
  • Any hours exceeding the AD-approved maximum threshold

Sport-Specific Rules Matter

Football: Non-Guaranteed Time begins after the regular season — up to 5 extra weeks for the full coaching staff.

Track & Field: All teams advance to sectionals automatically, so Non-Guaranteed Time begins after sectionals — only state qualifiers' week counts.

Strategic Talking Points

End the "Success Tax"

Coaches should not be financially penalized because their team wins. Advancement should increase professional value, not reduce effective hourly rate to zero.

Internal Retention

Fair compensation keeps our own teachers in coaching roles. External vendors don't know our students, culture, or community. Keep talent in-house.

Budgetary Certainty

The Pre-Set Calendar + Historical Average is proof of fiscal responsibility. Less than 0.1% of the budget, with zero risk of time-sheet padding.

Lead the Conference

Professionalizing the coaching contract positions BHS as a leader in the Dukane Conference — attracting top-tier talent and setting the standard.

Language & Framing

Avoid

  • "We want more money"
  • "Playoff pay" (implies only football)
  • "Retroactive raise"
  • "Budget increase"

Use Instead

  • "Indexing professional value to ensure internal retention"
  • "Non-Guaranteed Time" (equitable across all sports)
  • "Fiscal alignment with the Strategic Plan"
  • "Predictable, transparent compensation structure"
Frame every dollar as Strategic Alignment and Fiscal Predictability — not a demand, but a system improvement.
